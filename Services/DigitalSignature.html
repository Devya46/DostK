What Is DSC in Income Tax?

For those new to e-filing: DSC in income tax is your digital signature used to verify and approve income tax documents online. It authenticates your identity, binds digital data to the signatory, and ensures documents are not altered after signing. Essential for professional filers, audit reports, and mandatory for partnership firms, LLPs, and companies.

Use of DSC by Income Tax Dept

The Income Tax Department mandates DSC to validate authenticity, prevent tampering, and maintain signer identity. From ITRs to audit forms, DSC ensures faster processing and faceless assessment.

Class 3 DSC (Signing Only)

Only Class 3 Digital Signature Certificates are accepted for income tax e-filing after 2021. Legally valid under IT Act, they offer highest security and are required for forms like ITR-5, ITR-6, Audit reports (3CA/3CB/3CD), and foreign remittances.
